about

I’m Vedant, a second-year undergraduate researcher at IISER. My work blends theoretical physics, nonlinear mathematics, and astrophysical data. I study multi-morphogen reaction–diffusion systems, build JWST MIRI spectral extraction tools in Python, and develop geometric formulations for families of dynamic equations.

research

Turing & multi-morphogen systems

Studying combinatorial morphogen interactions and richer instability patterns beyond classical two-species reaction–diffusion theory.

Unified dynamic-equation geometry

A single formulation representing families of curves/equations using shared mensurational invariants such as area and integral structure.

JWST MIRI spectral extraction

Automating region selection & line identification from MIRI cubes using numpy, scipy, astropy, and reproducible pipelines.

Temporal energetic theory

Developing early theoretical notions of temporal energetic behavior across dynamical systems and model responses.

notes

Quantum Physics-1 notes, rewritten for conceptual clarity and junior accessibility. Structured PDF + annotated derivations + small runnable snippets.
