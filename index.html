about

I am Vedant, an undergraduate research student at IISER (joined 2024). my research blends theoretical physics, applied mathematics, and data-driven astrophysical analysis. I write notes, develop small open tools for spectral extraction, and explore theoretical frameworks that connect geometric/mensurational parameters to families of dynamic equations.

current roles: NASA Citizen Scientist (volunteer), course projects and independent research in reaction–diffusion systems, and JWST MIRI spectral work. I aim for concise, reproducible code and clear, slightly poetic exposition in my notes.

research — current & active

Turing & multi-morphogen dynamics

investigating emergent pattern formation when multiple morphogens interact. focusing on how combinatorial morphogen interactions change Turing instability thresholds and result in richer pattern vocabularies than classical two-species models.

unified dynamic-equation framework

developing a mathematical model that can represent families of plane curves and dynamic equations via shared geometric / mensurational parameters — a compact formulation that produces different shapes while preserving chosen invariants (area, certain integral constraints).

identifying spectral lines in JWST MIRI data

ISA summer project: automating region selection and spectral extraction from JWST MIRI cubes using python. tasks: region definition, background subtraction, line identification, and reproducible plotting/extraction pipelines for MIRI datasets.

temporal energetic behavior (theory development)

early-stage theoretical work relating 'temporal energetic' measures and their behavior across dynamical systems — aiming to formalize intuition into testable mathematical constructs.

notes & teaching

i maintain handwritten-to-digital notes for Quantum Physics-1 and other teaching materials; the Quantum Physics-1 notes are curated for juniors and formatted for open-source release. these notes accompany short, runnable python examples where relevant.
