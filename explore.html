Open Problem #001 Electrodynamic Interaction with Tuned Inductive Conductors

At an experiment conducted at the facility EDRS-IC (ElectroDynamics Research Sweden–India Coalition):

A deuteron moves with constant velocity $$ v = 7.2 \times 10^{6} \; km/h $$ through four conductors separated by distance \(d\).

The deuteron experiences a resistive electromagnetic force while travelling through the conductors. Each conductor is placed at $$ d = 10a $$ where \(a\) is the characteristic interaction length.

Given inductances $$ L_A = 0.44H,\; L_B = 0.88H,\; L_C = 0.176H,\; L_D = 0.36H $$

The circuits are tuned so that the magnetic energy stored in each inductor at the moment the particle crosses the center is equal.

The particle eventually strikes a perfectly reflecting mirror and rebounds. Determine the effective area \(A\) associated with the impulsive interaction.
